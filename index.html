Disaster Awareness
to be better prepared

Climate change, urban pressure and lack of disaster preparedness, are increasingly transforming natural hazards, such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions or tsunamis into disastrous events causing life and economic losses. The risk of disasters caused by natural hazards is rising.

Thus, our aim is to:

  1. Increase awareness about disasters happening around us
  2. Help make the general public better equiped to help themselves and others in times of disasters
  3. Encourage people, and provide them channels, to contribute towards disaster management

Dos and Don'ts
  • Cyclone
    1. Listen to radio or TV weather reports and alert everyone through a loud speaker or by going home to home.
    2. Identify safe shelter in your area. These should be cyclone resistant and also find the closest route to reach them. Move domestic animals to safety as well.
    3. Remain indoors until advised that the cyclone has passed away
    4. Do not venture into the sea. Stay Indoors and stand below the strongest part of the house if you have not moved to the cyclone shelter
    5. After the winds die down, wait for at least 1.5 hours before leaving your shelter.
    6. Do not use power points until they have been checked
    7. Be careful of fallen powers lines, damaged roads and houses, fallen trees.
  • Earthquake
    1. Make new constructions earthquake resistant in consultation of professional structural engineer, if possible.
    2. Conduct regular Mock Drills for school children.
    3. If outdoors, move to an open area away from all structure, especially building, bridges, trees and overhead power lines.
    4. Move cautiously, and check for unstable objects and other hazards above and around you.
    5. Check yourself for injuries. Help those in need.
  • Drought
    1. Never waste water, use it to water your indoor plants or garden especially in summers.
    2. Check all plumbing for leaks and get them repaired.
    3. Consider rainwater harvesting wherever practical.
    4. Choose appliances that are more energy and water efficient.
    5. Avoid letting the water run while brushing your teeth, washing your face or shaving etc.Use buckets instead of showers while bathing.
  • Flood
    1. Tune to your local radio/TV for warnings and advice.
    2. Keep dry food, drinking water and clothes ready.
    3. Move to high rise floor, raise furniture, clothing and valuables onto beds, tables etc. from getting wet.
    4. Turn off the main power supply. Do not use electrical appliances, which have been in floodwater.
    5. Do not get into water of unknown depth and current.
  • Landslide
    1. Avoid building houses near steep slopes, closes to mountain edges, near drainage ways or along natural erosion valleys.
    2. Listen for unusual sounds that might indicate moving debris, such as trees cracking or boulders knocking together.
    3. Move away from the path of a landslide or debris flow as quickly as possible.
    4. Stay away from the slide area as there may be danger of additional slides.
    5. Check for injured and trapped persons near the slide, without entering the direct slide area. Direct rescuers to their locations.
